# 🌿 Urban Parks & Citizen Wellbeing — Mairena del Aljarafe
Mixed-methods analysis of citizen perception of urban green spaces and institutional coordination
Mairena del Aljarafe · Seville Metropolitan Area · 2026

📌 What is this project?

This project presents a comprehensive mixed-methods study on how residents of Mairena del Aljarafe perceive, use and value their urban green spaces. Based on a survey of 225 citizens and in-depth ethnographic interviews, the analysis examines motivations for visiting parks, priorities for urban management, perceived quality of public spaces, and the level of trust in institutional coordination between the Ayuntamiento and the Junta de Andalucía.

The research pays special attention to the relationship between citizens feeling heard in new park projects and their confidence in local governance — a key dimension in urban anthropology and participatory planning. By integrating quantitative survey data with qualitative coding, this work offers both statistical robustness and contextual depth to understand socio-environmental dynamics in a growing metropolitan municipality.

🔍 Main Findings

* Socialisation (40%), emotional wellbeing (38%) and sport & health (36%) are the primary motivations for citizens visiting urban parks, highlighting their central role in community life and personal wellbeing.

* Maintenance and repairs (62%) emerges as the overwhelming priority for citizens, followed by shade and vegetation (33%) and cleanliness (25%). This reveals a strong demand for basic upkeep and climate adaptation measures.

* More than 84% of respondents (52.4% "Very important" + 32% "Important") consider coordination between Ayuntamiento and Junta as strategically relevant for the improvement of green spaces.

* There is a statistically visible relationship between perceived participation and institutional trust: citizens who feel their opinion was taken into account show a median score of 4 in institutional coordination, compared to a median of 3 among those who feel ignored.

* Clear territorial differences exist between neighbourhoods (e.g. Los Alcores, Simon Verde, Casco Antiguo), suggesting that urban interventions should be context-sensitive rather than one-size-fits-all.

* Qualitative analysis shows strong interconnections between problems of maintenance, lack of shade/vegetation and institutional governance issues.
